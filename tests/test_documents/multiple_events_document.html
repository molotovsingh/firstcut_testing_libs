COMPLEX LITIGATION TIMELINE - MULTIPLE EVENTS TEST

Case: TechCorp v. DataSystems Inc.

Case Number: 2024-CV-98765

Pre-Litigation Events

On January 5, 2024, TechCorp issued a cease and desist letter pursuant to the Digital Millennium Copyright Act.

DataSystems responded on January 20, 2024 denying all allegations and asserting fair use defenses under Section 107.

Settlement negotiations commenced on February 1, 2024 but broke down after three weeks of discussions.

Initial Pleadings

The complaint was filed on March 15, 2024 in the United States District Court pursuant to Federal Rule 3.

Service of process was completed on March 22, 2024 through certified mail to defendant's registered agent.

Defendant filed a motion to dismiss on April 12, 2024 under Federal Rule 12(b)(6) for failure to state a claim.

Plaintiff opposed the motion on April 26, 2024 with supporting memorandum of law.

The court denied the motion to dismiss on May 10, 2024 and ordered defendant to answer within 14 days.

Defendant's answer was filed on May 24, 2024 asserting twelve affirmative defenses.

Discovery Phase

The initial scheduling conference occurred on June 5, 2024 resulting in a comprehensive case management order.

Plaintiff served initial discovery requests on June 20, 2024 under Federal Rules 33 and 34.

Defendant responded to discovery on July 20, 2024 asserting various privilege objections.

A discovery dispute arose on August 1, 2024 regarding production of source code materials.

The court held a discovery conference on August 15, 2024 and ordered production within 30 days.

Depositions began on September 3, 2024 with plaintiff's CEO testifying for two days.

Defendant's CTO was deposed on September 17, 2024 pursuant to Federal Rule 30(b)(6).

Expert witness disclosures were exchanged on October 1, 2024 as required by Federal Rule 26(a)(2).

Motion Practice

Plaintiff filed a motion for partial summary judgment on October 15, 2024 under Federal Rule 56.

Defendant cross-moved for summary judgment on November 1, 2024 on all remaining claims.

Oral argument was scheduled for December 10, 2024 with briefing completed by November 25, 2024.

Settlement and Trial Preparation

A mandatory mediation session was conducted on November 15, 2024 pursuant to Local Rule 16.2.

Final pretrial conference is set for January 8, 2025 with all exhibits and witness lists due beforehand.

Jury selection is scheduled to begin on January 20, 2025 with trial estimated to last three weeks.

Post-trial motions, if any, must be filed within 30 days of verdict under Federal Rule 50.

Related Proceedings

A parallel state court action was filed on April 30, 2024 involving related contract claims.

The federal court stayed discovery in that case on May 15, 2024 pending resolution of this matter.

An injunctive relief hearing was held on June 30, 2024 but no temporary restraining order was issued.

Appeal deadlines will run from final judgment, which is expected by March 1, 2025.
